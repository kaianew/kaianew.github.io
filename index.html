About

I'm Kaia Newman, a first year Ph.D. student in Software Engineering in the Software & Societal Systems department at Carnegie Mellon University. I'm advised by Andrew Begel, and thus am a part of the VariAbility lab.

My research is on human-centered aspects of software engineering, such as program comprehension, developer productivity and wellbeing, communication in software teams, and neurodivergent developer accessibility. I am also interested in research aiding other software engineering researchers with their research (e.g., with tools that ensure rigor of empirical methods).

I completed my undergraduate degree in Computer Science at the University of Michigan. In my free time, I enjoy written communication, watching the reality TV show Survivor, doing crosswords, running, playing Magic: The Gathering, and easy reading.
